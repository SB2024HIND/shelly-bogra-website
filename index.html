Profile

Welcome to the profile of Shelly Bogra. I am a sustainability science enthusiast focusing on hidden systems and behaviors that are leading cause of unsustainable resource use and waste-generation. I primarily deal with spatially-explicit empirical investigations of resource-use at different scales. I am open to exploring new frontiers of sustainability assessments, especially in the context of developing nations.

For more details of my work, please visit Google Scholar profile.

Research Interests

  • Sustainability
  • Industrial Ecology: Environmentally extended input-output models, Development and Applications for direct and indirect footprints of supply chains
  • Ecological Economics: Emissions, behavior changes and technological options for mitigations
  • Ecosystem Services Accounting and Valuation
  • Systems thinking and life cycle impacts

Publications

  • A Water-Withdrawal Input–Output Model of the Indian Economy
  • Direct and indirect vulnerability of economic sectors to water scarcity: A hotspot analysis of the Indian economy
  • Greenhouse gas emissions of India's household food, apparel, mobility and energy consumption: Regional structure and inequality, and scenarios for 1.5°C climate stabilization
